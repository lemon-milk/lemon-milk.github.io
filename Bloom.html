Bali Bloom really tested me as a video creative. The project had all sorts of problems. Problems of the me-being-an-idiot persuasion. Among a serious cardiac stress test, a briefly misplaced memory card meant I thought I had lost half my footage. Panic mode engaged. It didn't disengage until about a week later when I actually found the card again and then realised I had actually lost all of my drone footage. Nice one you imbecile.

Didn't let that get in the way of creativity though. I pushed on and the edit came together quite well. Things were falling into place the way I pictured while filming. The creative high didn't last though; when it came time to colour correct and colour grade the previous overwhelming imbecilic feeling came back with great haste. No matter, continue to push through it like going through in labour in a delivery room.

Time to upload. Surely nothing can go wrong here. Nope, the imfamous YouTube compression algorithim back at it again. Compressing and pixelating my work into oblivion. Thanks YouTube. A quick Google search later and we're (kind of) back in business. Some pixelation and artefacting here and there but I'm happy with the overall result. All in all , good times - taught me a lot of my pitfalls during my filming process and what I should look out for. Also taught me a lot about YouTube codecs, because hey, that's always handy, right?
