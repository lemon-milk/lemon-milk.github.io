Shooting a red carpet premiere is never easy for me. Like a wedding, there's always an inherent pressure that comes along with the project. Unlike a weding though, there isn't always that clear focal point which you should devote your creative energy towards - eg bride and groom. The scope is always much larger; ontop of the usual environment and tone of the night that you're trying to capture, there's the local celebrities which attend, the wider audience members, the staff and crew as well as any additional events and activities throughout the night.

The Jurrassic World premiere was one such project that had to go through multiple edits to get right. My goal was to capture the grandure and spectacle of the night. The staff and crew had setup some amazing props and I didn't quite establish the feel I was going for in my first cut. Thankfully, I was much happier with my second passthrough which I was able to buil ontop of. A good lesson teaching me that sometimes the best way forward is to first take a step back.
